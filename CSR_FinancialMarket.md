- when we talk about an "exchange" in the context of futures contracts, we are indeed referring to a formal marketplace, like a stock exchange, where these contracts are traded. The exchange acts as an intermediary, ensuring that the contracts are standardized and that both parties fulfill their obligations. This structure helps to reduce risk and increase transparency in the trading process.

On the other hand, a forward contract is more like a private agreement between two parties, which can be arranged directly without the involvement of an exchange. Think of it as a handshake deal between two small businesses, where they agree on the terms without any formal oversight. This can lead to more flexibility, but it also carries more risk, as there's no intermediary to ensure that both parties stick to the agreement.
- forward, small scale and private, future large scale and public
